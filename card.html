Asian Elephants

The largest living land mammals, elephants are intelligent, social and vital to their ecosystems.

  • Scientific Name: Elephas maximus indicus
  • Average Weight: 2.25 to 5.5 tons
  • Average Lifespan: up to 60 years
  • Habitat: Forests

Asian elephants are the continent's largest terrestrial mammals. They can reach 6.4m in length and 3m at the shoulder, and weigh as much as 5 tons. They are smaller than African elephants and have proportionally smaller ears, which they keep in constant motion in order to cool themselves. They also have a single 'finger' on the upper lip of their trunks as opposed to African elephants, which have a second one on the lower tip.
